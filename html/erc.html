About the Programme

Civil society at the centre of regional cooperation

The overall objective of the WBF ERC Call for Proposals is to strengthen regional partnerships and enhance Civil Society Organisation (CSO) involvement in the Western Balkans, promoting democracy and active citizenship.

ERC is delivered under the EU/WBF Joint Action "Support to the Promotion of Civil Society Regional Actions in the Western Balkans (Phase II)" and is co-funded by the European Union. It channels grants directly to consortia of grassroots and established CSOs working together across borders.

Projects are built around three thematic areas of intervention — cultural cooperation, education and scientific exchange, and sustainable development — each carrying a strong commitment to the green agenda and environmentally responsible practice.

Every project must be implemented by a regional consortium and must mainstream at least three cross-cutting issues, from reconciliation and EU integration to digitalisation and the promotion of marginalised groups.

Eligibility

Who can apply — and how partnerships work

ERC is open to non-profit legal entities across the WB6, working together in regional consortia. There are three eligibility sets: applicants, project actions, and costs.

An eligible applicant must…

  • Be established in one or more of the WBF Contracting Parties (WB6)
  • Be non-profit-making
  • Be a legal entity of an eligible organisation type

The list of types is not exhaustive — other institutions may be considered eligible provided they respect the first three criteria.

Eligible organisation types

Both as Lead Applicant and as Project Partner
Civil Society Organisations (CSO/NGO)
Business associations
Enterprise & agriculture associations, chambers of commerce, tourism associations
Media associations
Organisations working with media, journalism and freedom of expression
Educational associations
Cultural-institution associations, sport associations, etc.

Partnership is the essential requirement

Every ERC project is delivered by a regional consortium. The partnership is what makes the action genuinely regional.

Minimum: 1 Lead Applicant + 3 Project Partners
  • The lead applicant plus a minimum of three project partners
  • Partnership parties from at least 4 different WBF Contracting Parties
  • An applicant may apply multiple times, but may be awarded only 1 grant as Lead and 1 grant as Partner (highest scored)
Find partners on the WBF Platform

Not eligible — neither as Lead nor as Partner

Beyond the standard exclusion grounds (bankruptcy, unpaid taxes/social contributions, fraud or corruption judgments, or actions already supported by the EU to avoid double funding), the following are not eligible under this specific call:

  • Profit-making companies
  • Political parties
  • Organisations dealing mostly/only with political or religious activities
  • Central & local government entities, units and directorates
  • Government-funded organisations (museums, universities, etc.)
Areas of Intervention

Three areas, one green agenda

Every ERC proposal must align with one or more of the three thematic areas, each integrating environmentally responsible practice and the green agenda.

01
Cultural Cooperation
Fostering regional cultural identity and intercultural dialogue through cooperation in sustainable initiatives, embracing a shared commitment to environmental stewardship and peaceful coexistence, and nurturing nature and cultural bonds for a harmonious, eco-conscious region.
Green agenda integrated
02
Education & Scientific Exchange
Advancing regional projects in education development, capacity building and mobility, with a commitment to the green agenda — integrating environmentally conscious practices into educational initiatives and promoting scientific exchanges for sustainable regional development.
Green agenda integrated
03
Sustainable Development
Promoting regional cohesion and institutional partnerships for sustainable economic development through integration of the green agenda into environmentally responsible practices, ensuring a harmonious balance between regional growth and ecological well-being.
Green agenda integrated
Eligible Activities

What ERC projects do

ERC funds three broad activity types, all targeting CSOs as direct beneficiaries.

Capacity Building
A planned, comprehensive effort to increase the effectiveness and viability of CSOs — ensuring they have the right framework and approach to achieve their core objectives.
Advocacy
Actions that build a more conducive environment for organisations — lobbying and advocating to decision-makers, media outreach, citizen education, and other forms of civic engagement.
Networking
Gatherings that foster professional and knowledge-sharing relationships among organisations — enabling them to exchange ideas, broaden connections, and discover opportunities.

The numbers behind your project

WBF funds up to 80% of total eligible costs; the consortium provides 20% co-financing. In-kind contribution is not eligible. The budget must be simple, realistic and based on real market prices.

Ineligible costs

  • Costs incurred before contract signature
  • Debts & debt service charges (interest)
  • Provisions for losses or future liabilities
  • Profit-making activities
  • Political or religious agenda activities
  • Costs already financed by another EU grant
  • Purchases of equipment, land or buildings
  • Currency exchange losses
  • Staff bonuses; customs & import duties
  • Fines, financial penalties, litigation costs
  • Taxes including VAT (except non-recoverable VAT, if declared)
  • Individual sponsorships for workshops/conferences
  • Alcohol & tobacco costs
  • Specific visibility expenditures & in-kind contribution
Double-financing rule: Based on EU regulations, WBF cannot support actions already supported by the European Union, to avoid duplicating EU funding of the same expenditures. The same activities and costs cannot be paid from two different awarded grants to the same entity.
Evaluation Process

How applications are assessed

Applications are examined and evaluated by WBF as Contracting Authority, with the assistance of external evaluators, in three steps.

1

Eligibility Check

Administrative screening against the eligibility and exclusion criteria. Applications that fail may be rejected without further review.

2

Evaluation of Applications

The WBF Secretariat scores eligible proposals against the Evaluation Grid and identifies the list of best projects.

3

Supporting Documents

Shortlisted applicants submit supporting documentation for verification before final award.

Evaluation Grid

Proposals are scored across these sections. For detailed sub-scores, see the full Guidelines on OGMS.
1 Partnership
2 Regional Relevance
3 Objective, Results & Activities
4 Capacity, Experience & Risk Management
5 Target Audience & Cross-Cutting Issues
6 Visibility
7 Financial Envelope
Decision-making: The list of best projects is approved by the WBF Council of Senior Officials, and the final list of awarded projects is endorsed by the WBF Conference of Ministers of Foreign Affairs. WBF strives to award projects in equal and balanced proportions across the Contracting Parties.

Supporting documents (shortlisted applicants)

  • Partnership Agreement (WBF format)
  • Scanned statute and registration certificate
  • Bank account verification (or latest statement) and latest annual financial statements of the Lead Applicant
  • Financial Capacity Form of the Lead Applicant (WBF format)
How to Apply

Applying & getting clarifications

Online submission via OGMS

Online submission through the Grant Management System is obligatory for this call. Organisations register to receive a unique protocol number.

OGMS is accessible via the WBF website at ogms.westernbalkansfund.org and wbfeuproject.org.

Register in OGMS in advance — don't wait until the last minute before the deadline to submit.

Questions & clarifications

  • Send clarification requests in writing to info@westernbalkansfund.org, up to 15 days before the deadline
  • No individual replies, and no phone inquiries
  • Replies are summarised and published on the WBF website no later than 10 days before the deadline
Visibility: winning applicants must follow the WBF Communication & Visibility Guidelines throughout the contract, and on every occasion publicise that the European Union has financed the action. Visibility must be ensured with no specific expenditures beyond the costs related to implementing the action's activities.
