About · The WB6

Member States

The Western Balkans Fund was founded — and is equally financed — by the six Contracting Parties of the Western Balkans. Each holds equal weight in the Conference of Ministers and the Council of Senior Officials.

Albania

Shqipëria · Host country of the WBF Secretariat
Capital: TiranaPopulation: 2.8MEU Candidate
View projects

Albania has hosted the WBF Secretariat in Tirana since the Host Country Agreement was signed in April 2018. The Albanian Ministry of Europe and Foreign Affairs holds Albania's seat on the Conference of Ministers.

Albanian organisations have been active participants in every GGI call since 2017, often as lead applicants in regional cooperation projects on culture, civil society and youth.

Kosovo* is one of the six Contracting Parties of WBF, with equal representation in all governance bodies. Kosovan organisations have made significant contributions, particularly in science, technology and youth cooperation.

Notable lead-applicant projects include the regional STEM and youth exchange initiative that connected participants from all six Contracting Parties.

Montenegro

Crna Gora · Црна Гора
Capital: PodgoricaPopulation: 0.6MEU Candidate
View projects

Montenegro joined the founding agreement in 2015 and was an early supporter of operationalising the Fund. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs holds the country's seat.

Montenegrin organisations are particularly active in inclusion, disability rights, cultural heritage and environmental protection projects, often partnering with neighbouring Albania and Serbia.

Serbia is the largest of the six Contracting Parties by population. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs holds Serbia's seat on the Conference of Ministers.

Serbian organisations are highly active across the full WBF portfolio — especially in civic transparency, journalism and media literacy, cultural heritage, and the Open Data initiative.
