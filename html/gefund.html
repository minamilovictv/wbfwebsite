01 / About the Programme

A regional fund for gender equality, born from the Berlin Process.

The Western Balkans Fund (WBF), established by the six WB Ministries of Foreign Affairs, has earmarked 15% of its core budget for gender equality initiatives. This commitment was formalised at the 2025 Western Balkans Leaders' Summit in London.

The Gender Equality Fund (GEFund) is the dedicated mechanism delivering that commitment. It is co-funded by the European Union (under EU Civil Society Facility & Media Programme, IPA III) and the Swiss Agency for Development and Cooperation (under "Fostering Inclusive Partnerships in the Western Balkans 2026–2029").

This first open call advances women's empowerment across the Western Balkans through regional civil society cooperation, advocacy, and innovative initiatives addressing social, economic, digital, environmental, and security challenges.

Partnership is the essential requirement.

Each project must be implemented by a regional consortium spanning at least three different Western Balkans Contracting Parties.

  • Minimum 3 different WBF Contracting Parties represented
  • One (1) Lead Applicant — the contract signatory with WBF
  • At least two (2) Project Partners from different WB6 CPs
  • Optional Associated Partners (no direct WBF funding)

03 / Priority Areas

Six priorities. One ambition.

Applicants must indicate the priority area their project addresses. Projects at the intersection of multiple priorities are encouraged.

Expected results

  • Advanced women's economic empowerment and participation in decision-making
  • Improved recognition of women's paid and unpaid work
  • Strengthened advocacy for gender-responsive economic policies and care economy investment

Expected results

  • Strengthened regional cooperation in addressing violence against women and girls
  • Improved support mechanisms and services for survivors of violence
  • Increased policy attention to sustainable funding for protection services

Expected results

  • Increased awareness of gender inequalities in digital technologies and AI
  • Strengthened participation of women and girls in STEM and digital innovation
  • Enhanced civil society engagement in shaping inclusive digital policies

Expected results

  • Increased integration of gender perspectives in environmental and disaster-response policies
  • Strengthened cooperation between CSOs, youth and environmental actors
  • Improved community resilience through gender-responsive climate approaches

Expected results

  • Strengthened regional cooperation in addressing anti-gender narratives and disinformation
  • Increased civic awareness and resilience to gender-based misinformation

Expected results

  • Stronger CSO engagement in peacebuilding and security policy discussions
  • Greater participation of women and youth in peace and security processes
  • Increased awareness of gender dimensions in migration, trafficking and humanitarian protection

06 / FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Q1. We cannot locate Annex A (Application Form), Annex B (Budget) and Annex C (Declaration by the Lead Applicant). Where can we find them?

All three annexes are embedded inside the WBF Online Grant Management System (OGMS) at wbfportal.org/applicant. Annex A and B are not provided as separate downloadable files.

Annex A — Application Form is completed directly inside OGMS by filling in the form fields once you create a new application.
Annex B — Budget of the Action is completed in the budget module of OGMS.
Annex C — Declaration by the Lead Applicant is downloaded from OGMS, signed and stamped, scanned, and re-uploaded.

Applications submitted by e-mail, fax or post will be rejected.

Q2. Can a blank Application Form be shared in Word format?

The official application can be prepared and submitted only via OGMS. We recommend opening an application early so your team can review the structure. You can draft content internally and transfer the final text into OGMS before the deadline.

Q3. Are Labour Unions eligible?

A labour union can be eligible only if it is registered as a non-profit legal entity in one of the WBF Contracting Parties and clearly fits within one of the listed categories. Political parties and religious institutions are not eligible in any role.

Q4. I am an individual professional. Can I apply directly?

Individuals and unregistered entities are not eligible. If you would like to contribute your expertise, connect with a registered eligible organisation via the WBF Partnership Platform.

Q5. Can an NGO be Lead Applicant with three schools from Serbia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Albania as Project Partners?

Yes. This composition satisfies the eligibility rules: an NGO is eligible as Lead Applicant; schools qualify as educational institutions; the partnership covers at least three different WBF Contracting Parties.

Q6. Can a single organisation implement the project on its own?

No. Partnership is an essential requirement. Each project must be implemented by a regional consortium of at least one Lead Applicant and at least two Project Partners from at least three different WBF Contracting Parties.

Q7. Where can we find the Partnership Agreement and the Financial Capacity Form?

Both documents are required only at Step 3 of the evaluation — after the application has been shortlisted. They are not part of the initial online submission. Shortlisted applicants will be notified through OGMS and provided with both templates.

Q8. We are a newly established organisation without annual financial statements. Will other documentation be accepted?

Equivalent documentation will be assessed by the WBF Secretariat on a case-by-case basis at Step 3. WBF cannot confirm in advance that any specific bundle will be deemed sufficient. We encourage you to submit your strongest possible application; if shortlisted, the Secretariat will follow up with you directly.

Q9. Is the grant paid to the Lead Applicant?

Yes. The grant is disbursed exclusively to the Lead Applicant, who then transfers funds to Project Partners per the Partnership Agreement. Disbursement is in two tranches: 80% upon contract signing, 20% upon approval of the final report.

Q10. Is VAT an eligible cost?

VAT that is non-recoverable under the applicable national VAT legislation is eligible and must be explicitly declared in Annex C. Recoverable VAT cannot be charged to the project. Each organisation should verify its VAT status under its national legislation before finalising the budget.

Q11. Our organisation employs researchers whose expertise corresponds to the project. Is there flexibility on the 25% staff-cost ceiling?

No. The 25% ceiling on Human Resource costs applies to all applicants and cannot be increased. It is calculated on the total amount granted by WBF. Permanent staff cannot be re-classified under "Experts" or "Volunteering" to bypass the HR ceiling.

Q12. We plan to appear as Project Partner in two separate proposals. What happens if both score positively?

An applicant may appear in several proposals as Project Partner but can only be awarded once in that role. At the evaluation stage, each proposal is assessed with the consortium as submitted. At the award stage, WBF cross-checks selected proposals and retains you only in the highest-scoring one. The remaining consortium must still satisfy the minimum eligibility rules; if it falls below the threshold, that proposal becomes ineligible at contracting stage.

Q13. Would a contemporary interdisciplinary theatre play addressing gender inequality be eligible?

In principle, yes — artistic and cultural formats are not excluded. Associations of culture are listed among the eligible organisation types and awareness-raising campaigns are explicitly listed among eligible activities. To be competitive, the proposal would need regional reach (at least 3 CPs), a clear gender-equality objective aligned with one of the six priority areas (DAC marker score 2), and sustainability beyond a single performance.

Q14. Can projects address internal/local migration challenges affecting women?

Yes. These themes fit within Priority 1 (Empowerment of Women in Economic & Public Life) and Priority 6 (Gender Equality, Peace & Security), which includes gender dimensions of migration among its expected results.

Q15. Is a woman-led micro-enterprise eligible to apply?

Eligible applicants must be non-profit-making legal entities. If the micro-enterprise is set up as a commercial entity it will not meet the non-profit-making criterion. However, it can contribute as an associated partner (without receiving project funds beyond justified per diem/travel costs), or be reached as a target group of project activities — for example, in initiatives advancing women's economic empowerment.
