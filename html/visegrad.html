Western Balkans–Visegrad Fellowships

Short-term academic fellowships for PhD students and doctoral holders, moving in both directions between the Western Balkans (WB6) and the Visegrad Group (V4). Conduct research, lecture, or study in archives and libraries across the two regions.

Jointly funded by the Western Balkans Fund and the International Visegrad Fund. Applications are submitted through the My Visegrad portal.

About the Programme

Academic mobility that connects two regions

The Western Balkans–Visegrad Fellowships allow WB6 citizens to apply for short-term fellowships at V4 research institutions (in Czechia, Poland, Hungary, or Slovakia). Reciprocally, V4 applicants can apply for fellowships at WB6 institutions (in Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Kosovo,* or Serbia).

The programme is jointly funded by the International Visegrad Fund and the Western Balkans Fund, reflecting a shared commitment to academic cooperation and people-to-people links between the two regions.

Fellowships run for a limited period of 2 to 10 weeks, during which the fellow conducts or participates in research, holds lectures or seminars at higher-education level, or studies sets of written or other materials in public archives or libraries.

The type of host institution is not prescribed but must be relevant to the nature of the fellowship — generally private or public higher-education institutions (colleges, universities, science academies), research bodies, libraries and archives, or laboratories.

Who Goes Where

Two directions, one fellowship

The fellowship works both ways — fellows always cross between the two regions, never within them.

Direction 1 — for WBF applicants
WB6 citizen V4 host
→ Czechia → Hungary → Poland → Slovakia

A doctoral researcher from Albania, BiH, Kosovo, Montenegro, North Macedonia or Serbia applies to a university, research body, library or lab in one of the four Visegrad countries.

Both ways
Direction 2 — for V4 applicants
V4 citizen WB6 host
→ Albania → BiH → Kosovo → Montenegro → N. Macedonia → Serbia

A doctoral researcher from Czechia, Hungary, Poland or Slovakia applies to a host institution in one of the six Western Balkans Contracting Parties.

Conditions & Documents

What you need to apply

The fellowship is designed for doctoral-level academics. Make sure you meet the conditions and have all four supporting documents ready.

Conditions

  • You are a full-time doctoral student or hold a doctoral degree (regardless of current affiliation)
  • You are a citizen of the WB6 or V4, applying to a host in the other region
  • Your host institution is relevant to the fellowship — university, science academy, research body, library, archive or lab
  • Your fellowship lasts between 2 and 10 weeks
  • The activity is research, lecturing/seminars, or study of archive/library materials

Required documents

1
Scanned valid ID document (passport)
2
Scan of the doctoral diploma with English translation if the original is not bilingual (translation need not be certified), or a proof of enrolment in a doctoral programme
3
Letter of invitation signed/stamped by the host institution, detailing the length, exact nature, and intended outcomes of the fellowship — written in English
4
Letter of recommendation from the applicant's home institution or a relevant expert (for non-affiliated applicants) — written in English
Funding

What the fellowship covers

Support is a lump-sum scholarship, with an optional travel grant on top. Both are calculated up front and paid against confirmed arrival and reporting.

Scholarship
€500 / week
A lump-sum scholarship for the full duration of the fellowship, paid in two instalments.
  • 1st instalment (on confirmed arrival)~80%
  • 2nd instalment (on summary report)~20%
  • Minimum duration2 weeks
  • Maximum duration10 weeks
Optional travel grant
Up to €500
A separate lump-sum travel grant, calculated on estimated travel costs.
  • Maximum amount€500
  • BasisEstimated cost
  • Claimed automatically?No

Note: the travel grant must be requested separately — it cannot be automatically claimed as part of the scholarship.

How to Apply

Applying through My Visegrad

Applications for this fellowship are submitted through the International Visegrad Fund's My Visegrad portal, not through WBF's OGMS.

1

Secure your host & documents

Arrange a host institution in the other region and obtain a signed letter of invitation. Prepare your ID, doctoral diploma (or enrolment proof), and a letter of recommendation — all in English.

2

Apply on My Visegrad

Submit your application through my.visegradfund.org before the deadline. Upload all four required documents and specify the precise length and nature of your fellowship.

This is a joint programme — applications and full official guidelines are managed by the International Visegrad Fund. For questions, you can consult the IVF scholarship FAQ or contact the WBF Secretariat at info@westernbalkansfund.org.
