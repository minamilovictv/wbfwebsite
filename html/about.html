Western Balkans Fund

An international organisation dedicated to regional cooperation, people-to-people connectivity, and the European integration of the Western Balkans.

Our Mandate

What is the Western Balkans Fund?

The Western Balkans Fund (WBF) is an international organization located in Tirana, Albania, founded by the governments of Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Kosovo*, North Macedonia, Montenegro and Serbia. Its establishment, as an all-inclusive and regionally owned initiative, is considered a clear sign of a new spirit of cooperation taking root in the Balkans.

The Agreement for the creation of WBF was signed by the Ministers of Foreign Affairs of the WB6 in November 2015. The Fund became operational on October 1, 2017, following the completion of ratification procedures by all parliaments of its constitutive members.

WBF is financed by the six Contracting Parties through equal contributions. The first appointed Executive Director, elected by the WBF Conference of Ministers of Foreign Affairs, was Mr. Gjergj Murra, a career diplomat from Albania. The Secretariat is based in Tirana and is composed of experts from across the Western Balkans region.

WBF aims to promote cooperation and shared values among citizens, civil society organizations, and people-to-people contacts by providing funding for small and medium-sized projects in key areas of regional cooperation.

*This designation is without prejudice to positions on status and is in line with UNSCR 1244 and the ICJ Opinion on the Kosovo Declaration of Independence.

Our Mission

The Western Balkans Fund (WBF) is dedicated to promoting reconciliation, strengthening regional cooperation, and fostering closer ties among the Contracting Parties of the Western Balkans. Through its initiatives, the Fund contributes to building trust, dialogue, and sustainable partnerships across the region.

Values

The Western Balkans Fund operates in full alignment with the vision of its founding members, supporting the principles of reconciliation, regional solidarity, and the shared European future of all Contracting Parties. Our work is guided by cooperation, inclusiveness, transparency, and mutual respect.
