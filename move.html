About the Programme

People-to-people connections at the heart of regional cooperation

MOVE Grants are WBF's dedicated mobility mechanism — part of the Diversified Grant Portfolio (DGP) — designed for the individual changemaker who wants to cross a border, learn something, collaborate, and bring it back home.

Unlike WBF's larger calls for organisational proposals, MOVE is built around the belief that direct, in-person exchange — academic, artistic, professional — is irreplaceable for building trust across the Western Balkans. A researcher visiting a lab in another country, a journalist attending a regional media forum, an activist participating in a residency: these are the connections that accumulate into something larger.

Each MOVE grantee commits to a structured mobility activity with a formal host institution in another WB6 Contracting Party. After the visit, they share their experience — in their networks, on social media, through WBF's platforms — becoming ambassadors for regional cooperation.

The scheme opens twice a year — typically February and October — with a 30-day application window and results within 40 working days. Grants range from €500 to €3,000 and cover up to 100% of eligible costs.

Areas of Intervention

Three areas. Unlimited forms of mobility.

Your mobility initiative must align with at least one area and address one or more cross-cutting issues: gender equality, human rights & rule of law, environmental sustainability, inclusion of minorities, or digitalisation & innovation.

Cultural Cooperation
Enhancing regional cultural identity and intercultural dialogue through exchange, heritage preservation and mutual understanding.
  • Regional cultural festivals, exhibitions & performances
  • Joint artistic productions — theatre, film, music, visual arts
  • Artist residencies and immersive creative programs
  • Cultural heritage preservation and documentation projects
  • Intercultural dialogue workshops and youth forums
  • Digital cultural platforms and multimedia projects
Education & Scientific Exchange
Supporting capacity building, academic and professional mobility, joint research and scientific collaboration across the WB6.
  • Study and research exchanges at universities and labs
  • Workshops, seminars and professional development programs
  • Academic and professional internships or work placements
  • Conferences, symposiums and academic presentations
  • Joint research projects and scientific publications
  • Field research and data collection across the region
Sustainable Development
Advancing regional cohesion through partnerships on sustainable economic development, environmental protection and social inclusion.
  • Environmental research — climate, biodiversity, resources
  • Sustainability training, workshops and capacity-building
  • Study visits to green economy organisations
  • Social inclusion programs for marginalised communities
  • Policy dialogues on sustainable development
  • Collaborative advocacy projects for sustainable practices
Eligibility

Built for individuals, not organisations

MOVE is the only WBF grant scheme designed for individual applicants. Each person applies alone and is personally responsible for their mobility initiative and its reporting.

You qualify if…

  • You are at least 18 years old on the application deadline
  • You are a citizen of one of the WB6 Contracting Parties (Albania, BiH, Kosovo, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Serbia)
  • You are an established professional (artist, researcher, journalist, activist, etc.), employed/affiliated with a legal organisation, or currently enrolled as a student
  • You have no criminal record
  • You have a good command of English
  • Your mobility crosses into at least one other WB6 Contracting Party (not your home country)
  • You do not hold an active MOVE Grant at the time of application

Partnership with host institution

You must establish a formal partnership with the host institution in another WB6 CP — the mobility must be grounded in a structured, mutually agreed framework, not an informal visit.
If shortlisted, you will need to provide an official invitation letter from the host institution, or a confirmed registration or acceptance into a program, course, residency or workshop.
One application per Call. An applicant cannot hold more than one active MOVE Grant at the same time. A previous grant must be completed or formally closed before applying again.
Activities should primarily take place within the Western Balkans region. Activities outside the region (e.g. in the EU) may be considered in exceptional, justified cases if they promote the WB6 as a cooperative region.

Only individual applications are eligible. Applications to support the mobility of a group of individuals travelling together will not be supported. If you are planning a group initiative, consider the GGI Grants scheme instead.

Ineligible costs — not covered under MOVE

  • Costs incurred before signing the grant contract
  • Salaries, fees or consultancy payments (HR costs)
  • Indirect / overhead costs
  • Double-funded expenses (already covered elsewhere)
  • Personal expenses & entertainment
  • Luxury or extravagant purchases
  • Debt payments or interest charges
  • Profit-making or political/religious activities
  • Land or building purchases
  • Currency exchange losses
  • Fines, penalties or legal charges
  • Provisions for future losses or liabilities
Evaluation Process

How applications are assessed

All applications are evaluated in four steps. Results are communicated within 40 working days of the call deadline.

1

Administrative & Eligibility Check

Applications are screened against formal eligibility criteria before any quality assessment. Missing or incorrect information can lead to rejection at this stage.

  • Applicant is 18+ and a WB6 citizen
  • Application submitted within the deadline (in English)
  • No active WBF grant held at time of application
  • Mobility involves at least two Contracting Parties
  • Budget does not exceed €3,000
  • Declaration by the Applicant (Annex C) filled in and signed
2

Technical Evaluation — Scoring Grid (100 points)

Applications passing Step 1 are scored on relevance, quality and operational feasibility. A minimum of 55 points is required to proceed.

Section Criterion Guiding principle Score
A — Project Description & Coherence 25 pts
Project description Addresses a well-defined topic and explains it clearly 1–10
Duration justification & realistic activities Activities are proportionate and achievable within the timeframe 1–5
Defined roles Applicant's and host institution's roles are clearly defined and justified 1–10
B — Regional Relevance & Call Objective 50 pts
Regional relevance & dimension Addresses common regional challenges; mobility dimension is clear and significant 1–20
Contribution to MOVE Main objective contributes to WBF MOVE Programme within a chosen area of intervention 1–10
Personal growth potential Project allows personal growth to explore, create, learn, participate or connect regionally 1–10
Sustainability & follow-up Proposal includes follow-up activity and sustainability elements 1–5
Introduction of new practices Project introduces new practices, relevant topics and/or innovative solutions 1–5
C — Operational 15 pts
Financial planning & efficiency Financial plan reflects value for money, transparency and efficient use of resources 1–10
Communication & visibility Communication approach is appropriate to reach visibility objectives 1–5
Applications scoring below 55 points will not be recommended to the Evaluation Panel.
3

Evaluation Panel

The WBF Evaluation Panel reviews high-ranking proposals per Contracting Party and votes on the most successful applications. A shortlist and reserve list are endorsed by the WBF Council of Senior Officials and the Conference of Ministers of Foreign Affairs.

4

Verification of Supporting Documents

Provisionally selected applicants must submit:

  • Government-issued ID (passport or ID card)
  • Criminal record clearance from a government agency
  • Employment/enrolment confirmation letter (if applicable)
  • Official invitation letter from the host institution in a WB6 CP
  • Partnership agreement between home and host (if applicable)

Any inconsistency between submitted documents and declarations may result in rejection. The next best-placed application on the reserve list would then be awarded.

How to Apply

Three steps to submit your application

All applications are submitted in English through the WBF Online Grant Management System (OGMS). Incomplete or handwritten applications will not be accepted.

1

Register on OGMS

Create your account on the WBF portal at wbfportal.org/applicant. Once registered, open a new MOVE Grant application to see all required fields — you can save progress at any time.

2

Complete your application

Annex A (Application Form) and Annex B (Budget) are completed directly in OGMS. Annex C (Declaration by the Applicant) is downloaded from OGMS, signed, scanned and re-uploaded. Draft your content offline first, then transfer to OGMS before the deadline.

3

Submit & confirm

Submit before the deadline (midnight CET). You will receive an automatic confirmation email with a protocol number and a short survey link that must be completed. If no email arrives within 48 hours, contact info@westernbalkansfund.org.

Planning ahead: if you require a visa for your destination country, WBF strongly encourages you to schedule your mobility start at least two months after receiving the official award notification, to allow sufficient time for administrative preparations. The call opens twice a year — applicants not selected may apply again in future calls.
